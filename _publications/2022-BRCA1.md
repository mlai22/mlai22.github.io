excerpt: 'We employed Kaplan–Meier analysis, Cox proportional hazards regression, and a cure survival model to evaluate the relationship between BRCA1 expression and distant metastasis-free survival in ER+ breast cancer. The cure survival model enabled differentiation between early metastatic events and long-term metastasis-free survivors, providing a more nuanced assessment of prognosis than conventional survival analysis alone. We found that high BRCA1 mRNA expression was associated with increased tumor proliferation (luminal B subtype), elevated expression of proliferation markers (MKI67 and PCNA), and a higher risk of early distant metastasis, suggesting that BRCA1 expression may serve as a prognostic biomarker for aggressive ER+ breast cancer and may influence tumor progression through interactions with estrogen receptor signaling.'
