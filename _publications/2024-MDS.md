excerpt: "We applied a Bayesian integrative multi-omics clustering approach to identify molecularly distinct subgroups of Parkinson's disease (PD) using cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) and urine proteomics, blood transcriptomics, and plasma metabolomics from the Parkinson's Progression Markers Initiative (PPMI) cohort. Statistical analyses included iClusterBayes for Bayesian integrative clustering of multi-omics data, ordinal logistic regression for clinical severity outcomes, logistic regression for binary outcomes, and linear mixed-effects models to evaluate longitudinal disease progression. The study identified two molecular PD subclusters with distinct genetic composition, clinical progression, metabolomic profiles, and biomarker characteristics, demonstrating that multi-omics integration can reveal biologically meaningful PD subtypes and improve patient stratification for precision medicine."
