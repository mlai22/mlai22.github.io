excerpt: 'We analyzed longitudinal treatment responses using generalized estimating equation regression models for repeated measures, allowing assessment of temporal changes while accounting for within-subject correlation. Interaction terms were incorporated to evaluate differential treatment effects by BMI subgroup, and treatment effects were reported with 95% confidence intervals, providing robust estimates of endocrine and metabolic changes following metformin therapy. Our findings indicate that BMI modifies the therapeutic response to metformin in polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), highlighting the importance of phenotype-specific treatment strategies in clinical trials.'
